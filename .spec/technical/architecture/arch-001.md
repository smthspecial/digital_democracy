## Overview

The Digital Democracy System is organized as thirteen functional layers spanning identity, eligibility, problem and proposal handling, expert review, deliberation, budgeting, voting, implementation, oversight, civic knowledge, delegation, and constitutional protection. Political authority originates from and remains with citizens; institutions facilitate, provide expertise, and execute, but do not own decision-making authority.

## Details

Layers: (1) Citizen Identity, (2) Sphere of Impact, (3) Problem Registry, (4) Proposal Marketplace, (5) Expert Review, (6) Public Deliberation, (7) Budget, (8) Voting, (9) Implementation, (10) Public Oversight, (11) Civic Knowledge, (12) Delegated Expertise, (13) Constitutional. Each layer maps to an epic (EPIC-001..EPIC-012, with Problem Registry and Proposal Marketplace combined under EPIC-003). Cross-cutting integrity concerns are handled by the governance-infrastructure layers in ARCH-003. See related decisions ADR-004 (sphere of impact), ADR-001 (layered accountability).
