## Context

Binary yes/no framing forces complex issues into simplistic choices and amplifies polarization.

## Decision

Support multiple alternatives per decision using ranked-choice, approval, preference-scoring, or comparative-ranking methods, selecting broadly acceptable solutions rather than maximizing division.

## Consequences

Lower polarization and richer expression of preferences. Cost: more complex ballots and tallying, and a need for voter education on methods. Realizes FR-032 and supports FR-018.
