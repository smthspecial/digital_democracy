## Context

Direct majorities can harm minorities; fundamental rights must not be subject to ordinary votes (tyranny of the majority).

## Decision

Place fundamental rights in a constitutional layer outside ordinary voting; require supermajorities, elevated participation, expert assessment, and extended deliberation for constitutional change, and allow constitutional review to block rights-violating proposals.

## Consequences

Rights are protected regardless of majority opinion. Cost: some decisions are deliberately harder and slower to change. Realizes FR-058, FR-059.
