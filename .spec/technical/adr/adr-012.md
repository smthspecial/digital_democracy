## Context

Debates that start from competing solutions produce conflict before common ground is found, and repeatedly re-litigate established facts.

## Decision

Require proposals to begin by agreeing constraints and to explicitly separate areas of agreement (facts, objectives) from areas of disagreement (methods) before and during deliberation.

## Consequences

More productive deliberation and less polarization. Cost: an added up-front structuring step. Realizes FR-029, FR-033 and supports FR-034.
