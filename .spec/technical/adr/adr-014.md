## Context

The system had no specified authentication layer. Every governance action (casting a ballot, submitting a protocol approval, publishing an expert assessment) requires that the system continuously trusts the identity of the actor making the request — not just at account creation. Account takeover, session hijacking, and credential theft are realistic threats for a system handling national governance decisions.

Three problems to solve simultaneously:
1. Proving you are who you claim to be on every request, not just at registration.
2. Applying a higher bar of assurance before irreversible or high-stakes actions.
3. Keeping the legal identity document (passport, national ID) out of the operational system while still being able to re-verify a returning user.

## Decision

Build an internal `auth-service` (SRV-017) that owns session lifecycle, MFA factor management, and step-up authentication. Do not delegate to an external identity provider — civic identity is the foundation of political authority and must not depend on a third-party commercial service.

### Session model

Short-lived access tokens (15-minute TTL) paired with a rotating refresh token stored server-side in `TBL-037`. The refresh token is bound to device fingerprint and IP subnet. A mismatch triggers an anomaly event and forces re-authentication.

### MFA tiers

Three assurance tiers applied based on action sensitivity:

| Tier | Required for | MFA requirement |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| T1 — Read | Viewing public data, browsing proposals | Session token only (no MFA) |
| T2 — Standard write | Post argument, declare preference, endorse problem, accept assignment | Active session + at least one enrolled MFA factor verified within the last 12 hours |
| T3 — High-stakes write | Cast ballot, submit approval decision, propose/activate protocol change, revoke identity | Fresh MFA challenge within the last 5 minutes, with biometric factor required if enrolled |

### MFA factors (stored in TBL-038)

| Factor type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| `totp` | Time-based one-time password (TOTP / authenticator app) |
| `passkey` | WebAuthn passkey (hardware key or platform authenticator) |
| `facial` | Facial biometric — live match against the reference embedding captured during identity verification (DP-002). Liveness detection required; static photo rejected. |

At least one factor must be enrolled before a citizen can perform any T2 action. Facial biometric enrollment is strongly recommended (displayed as such in the UI) and required before T3 actions when no passkey is enrolled.

### Biometric handling

The facial reference embedding is captured once during identity verification (DP-002) and stored in encrypted form in `mfa_factor.biometric_embedding` — never as a raw image. The raw verification photo is discarded after embedding extraction. The embedding is not shared with any other service. Re-verification during step-up sends a fresh liveness capture to auth-service only; no embedding leaves the service.

### Anomaly detection

auth-service monitors for: login from a new device/country, multiple failed MFA attempts, token reuse, rapid sequential logins, and concurrent sessions from distinct geographies. On anomaly: session is suspended, the citizen is notified via notification-service, and re-authentication with T3 assurance is required to restore access.

## Consequences

- Account takeover requires compromising both the session token and a physical factor (passkey or enrolled face); replay attacks are blocked by token TTL and liveness detection.
- The biometric embedding never leaves auth-service; no other service or operator can extract the citizen's face.
- Short TTL access tokens mean a stolen token is valid for at most 15 minutes before expiry.
- Cost: added infrastructure (biometric matching pipeline), enrollment friction for citizens, and complexity in the step-up flow for high-stakes actions.
- Realizes NFR-007 (security as critical infrastructure), NFR-002 (anti-fraud), NFR-006 (privacy), and fills the authentication gap identified in AUTH-009.
