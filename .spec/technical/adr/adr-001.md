## Context

Any governance system must be implemented, maintained, and updated. A single final authority can be captured; no authority at all leaves the system unstable or unupdatable (the Authority Paradox).

## Decision

Decompose power across four independent layers — Protocol (rules), Implementation (operators), Audit (verification), and Citizen (sovereign decisions) — with limited, non-overlapping control. No single layer can modify, execute, and validate a governance action.

## Consequences

No irreversible power concentration is possible. Coordination across independent actors is required for powerful actions, is observable, and leaves permanent evidence. Cost: higher coordination overhead and more complex change management. Realizes FR-061, FR-067 and NFR-008.
