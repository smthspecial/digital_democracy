## Context

Trusted identity is the foundation for one-person-one-vote and eligibility. Weak identity undermines every other mechanism; over-strong identity risks surveillance.

## Decision

Bind each civic identity to a verified legal identity via national identity systems, passports, or government credentials, while keeping government identifiers and personal data out of public view and out of operator surveillance reach.

## Consequences

Strong fraud and foreign-interference resistance with preserved privacy. Cost: dependence on external identity authorities and careful privacy engineering. Realizes FR-001, FR-002 and NFR-002, NFR-006.
