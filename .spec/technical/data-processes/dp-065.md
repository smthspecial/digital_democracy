## Description

Selects and activates a review body panel for a specific governance dispute. Review bodies are independent, case-specific, and may not share membership across concurrent cases.

### Trigger events and panel requirements

| Trigger | Panel size | Competency requirement | Term |
|---------|-----------|----------------------|------|
| Scope challenge (FR-010) | 3 members | None | Until case resolved, max 30 days |
| Competency appeal (FR-024) | 3–5 members | Domain expertise match required for at least 2 members | Until case resolved, max 60 days |
| Constitutional review (FR-058) | 5 members | Legal/constitutional domain expertise preferred | Until decision issued, max 90 days |
| Deadlock resolution (FR-034) | 3 members | Domain relevance | Until deadlock resolved |

### Selection flow

1. governance-role-service receives the dispute event with its type and domain.
2. Build eligible pool: active citizens with `citizen.status=active`, no active governance_role in another concurrent review body case, no COI in the dispute domain (DP-033 check).
3. For cases requiring domain expertise: weight selection toward citizens with `competency.status=active` in the dispute domain, while preserving random base selection (ADR-009).
4. Select panel from eligible pool using weighted randomization.
5. For each selected citizen: run DP-062 (acceptance handshake). Panel is not activated until all seats are accepted.
6. If a citizen declines or times out: replace with next candidate from the eligible pool.
7. Write `governance_role` rows (TBL-032, role_type=review_body, randomized=true, term_end=case_deadline).
8. Write `civic_assignment` rows (TBL-024) scoped to the dispute's target ref.
9. Emit to audit.append (DP-036).

### Independence guarantee

No two members of the same review body panel may share an employer, organizational affiliation, or family relationship (as declared in COI records). governance-role-service checks COI declarations before finalizing selection. A undeclared relationship discovered after activation triggers immediate replacement and a COI misconduct record.

### Decision and term end

On case resolution: review body members must publish a public rationale (FR-011). Unexplained decisions are structurally invalid — the system will not accept a review outcome without a `decision_rationale` field. On publication: `governance_role.status → expired`, `civic_assignment.status → completed`. DP-050 enforces expiry at `term_end` even if no decision has been published (escalates to a governance integrity alert).
