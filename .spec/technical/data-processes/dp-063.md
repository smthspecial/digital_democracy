## Description

Governs the appointment of operator credentials. Operators run implementation infrastructure and have elevated write access to the ledger and project milestones. No single actor can appoint an operator unilaterally.

### Appointment flow

1. A protocol_council member (AUTH-008) submits an operator appointment proposal — this is a protocol-layer action, not a standard governance proposal. It includes: the organization's legal name, the scope of operator authority (which services/projects), and the proposed term.
2. The appointment enters the multi-approval workflow (DP-035) with required approvals:
   - `citizen_supermajority` — via a vote session (SRV-008) limited to citizens in the affected jurisdiction.
   - `audit_confirmation` — an active audit body (AUTH-003) reviews the appointment for conflicts.
   - `body_endorsement` — a review_body panel (AUTH-007) endorses the appointment.
3. All three approvals must be granted independently (TBL-033). No single actor may supply more than one approval type.
4. After all approvals: delayed execution (FR-065) — a public visibility window of 14 days during which any citizen may file a constitutional challenge (DP-034).
5. After delay elapsed and no successful block: DP-043 triggers this process.
6. governance-role-service writes `governance_role` row (TBL-032, role_type=operator, layer=implementation) for the organizational credential.
7. Operator credentials issued via a separate credential issuance process (not civic identity). Credentials are tied to organizational role, not to any individual citizen's civic identity.
8. All steps emitted to audit.append (DP-036).

### Revocation

Operator credentials may be revoked by the same multi-approval path. Immediate suspension (pending full revocation) may be triggered by a unanimous audit body finding, subject to DP-035 fast-track approval.
