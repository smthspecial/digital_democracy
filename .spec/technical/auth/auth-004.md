## Description

A citizen randomly selected to perform specific governance review tasks: proposal review, expertise verification, and budget oversight. Reviewers are the front line of distributed governance work, ensuring proposals meet quality and constraint requirements before advancing. Selection is weighted by impact relevance, competency, and workload balance.

## Acquisition

Generated by civic-duty-service (SRV-009) via DP-040 when a review task is created. Assignment appears as a `civic_assignment` (type=`proposal_review`, `expertise_verification`, or `budget_oversight`) with a `due_at` deadline. The underlying `governance_role` record (type=reviewer) is created alongside.

## Capabilities (additive over AUTH-001)

| Capability | Condition | FR |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Access assigned review tasks | Active governance_role (type=reviewer, within term) | FR-050 |
| Submit review findings on assigned proposals | Active term, within assigned scope | FR-050 |
| Participate in expertise verification review | Active term, relevant competency (advisory) | FR-022 |
| Submit budget oversight observations | Active term, assigned to budget_oversight task | FR-036 |

## Restrictions

- Authority is scoped to the specific `target_ref` in the `civic_assignment`. Reviewers cannot act outside their assigned scope.
- Findings are advisory contributions to the governance process; they do not constitute binding decisions.
- Cannot approve protocol changes, certify votes, or execute implementation decisions.
- COI exclusion applies: a reviewer with a conflict in the domain of the assigned task is excluded (FR-063, DP-033).

## Term and rotation

- Role is task-scoped. `governance_role.term_end` corresponds to `civic_assignment.due_at`. On completion or abandonment the role expires.
- Repeated assignment is allowed but constrained by workload balancing (DP-052) and the 2-4 hours/month quota (FR-051).
