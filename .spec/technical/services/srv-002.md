## Responsibility

Owns the jurisdiction hierarchy, residency records, and citizen-jurisdiction memberships. Provides the sphere-of-impact model underpinning all eligibility decisions: which citizens may vote on, deliberate about, or be assigned to a given proposal. Jurisdiction boundaries are immutable references to externally published definitions; this service stores pointers, not boundaries.

## Owned Tables

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| TBL-003 jurisdiction | Primary owner — hierarchy, scope levels, boundary refs |
| TBL-004 residency | Primary owner — verified residency periods |
| TBL-005 jurisdiction_membership | Primary owner — simultaneous multi-jurisdiction memberships |

## Operations

| Operation | Mode | Process |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Read jurisdiction tree | sync (read) | — |
| Check citizen eligibility for proposal | sync (read) | called by voting-service during DP-025 |
| Verify residency at vote time | sync (read) | FR-012 check on demand |

## Key rules

- Jurisdiction boundaries are `boundary_ref` pointers to externally published, independently reviewed definitions (ADR-004). They are never stored as mutable data.
- `scope_level` determines which threshold and voting method applies (FR-014, FR-042).
- A citizen may hold simultaneous `jurisdiction_membership` records for nested jurisdictions (FR-013): neighborhood + city + region + national.
- Residency `start_date` is used for minimum-period enforcement (FR-012); the check runs at vote time, not at enrollment time.
- Scope-level changes to a jurisdiction require protocol-layer approval (DP-035, FR-067).

## Dependencies

- Read by: `voting-service` (eligibility), `proposal-service` (scope assignment), `civic-duty-service` (assignment weighting)
- Emits to: `audit-service` (DP-036) on any structural change
