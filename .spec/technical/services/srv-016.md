## Responsibility

Provides transparent, advisory-only AI assistance for policy synthesis: summarizing citizen preferences, identifying shared objectives, detecting conflicts, generating alternative framings, and estimating tradeoffs. Has no decision-making authority. All output is clearly labeled as AI-generated and subject to human review and rejection (ADR-007, FR-031).

## Owned Tables

None. AI-generated advisory outputs are stored in a separate advisory store (outside the core governance schema) and displayed as labeled suggestions. They are never written back to any owned table of another service.

## Operations

| Operation | Mode | Process |
|-----------|------|---------|
| AI policy synthesis | async | DP-037 (triggered by argument/preference volume threshold) |

## Key rules

- DP-037 fires when the count of new `deliberation_argument` or `preference` rows on a proposal crosses a configurable threshold. The worker reads those tables (read-only) and produces advisory output.
- Advisory output is explicitly labeled "AI-generated analysis — advisory only, subject to human review." Labels are mandatory and non-removable in any UI that displays AI output (ADR-007).
- The service has no write access to any core governance table. It cannot create proposals, constraints, arguments, assessments, or any record that affects governance state.
- AI output is subject to public review by any citizen. Any citizen may flag AI output as biased or misleading; flagged output is reviewed by the review_body.
- Model provenance, version, and training data summary are published alongside each synthesis output for auditability (ADR-013).
- AI synthesis can be disabled by protocol-layer decision at any time without affecting the governance process, since it is advisory infrastructure, not a governance gate.

## Dependencies

- Reads from: `deliberation-service` (deliberation_argument, preference — read only)
- Emits to: advisory output store (not governance schema), `audit-service` (DP-036 on synthesis execution)
