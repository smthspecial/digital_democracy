## Responsibility

Owns the full lifecycle of civic identities and verification records. Enforces the one-person-one-identity invariant, binds civic handles to verified legal identities, and manages identity status transitions (active, suspended, revoked). Is the authoritative source of truth for whether a given citizen may participate.

## Owned Tables

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| TBL-001 citizen | Primary owner — creates, updates status, holds public_handle and legal_identity_hash |
| TBL-002 identity_verification | Primary owner — stores verification attempts and outcomes |

## Operations

| Operation | Mode | Process |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Register civic identity | sync | DP-001 |
| Submit verification evidence | sync | DP-002 |
| Duplicate identity detection | async | DP-024 |
| Identity revocation cascade | async | DP-042 |
| Duplicate identity sweep | cron | DP-056 |

## Key rules

- `legal_identity_hash` is a salted hash of the government identifier; the raw identifier is never stored (NFR-006).
- A citizen transitions to `active` only after at least one `identity_verification` record reaches `status=verified`.
- Identity revocation (FR-006) requires multi-approval (DP-023 → DP-035) before DP-042 executes; no single operator can revoke unilaterally (FR-007).
- Every status change is emitted to audit.append (DP-036).

## Dependencies

- Emits to: `audit-service` (DP-036), `notification-service` (DP-039)
- Read by: `voting-service` (eligibility check), `jurisdiction-service` (residency), `governance-role-service` (role assignment)
