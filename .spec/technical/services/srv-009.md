## Responsibility

Distributes governance work fairly across the citizen population. Generates randomized, weighted civic assignments (proposal reviews, audits, expertise verifications, budget oversight). Tracks participation scores and quotas. Manages inactivity escalation. Refreshes audit pools. Ensures no citizen is overloaded and no governance task goes unassigned.

## Owned Tables

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| TBL-024 civic_assignment | Primary owner — per-citizen task records with type, target, due date, and status |
| TBL-025 participation_record | Primary owner — monthly scores, quotas, exemptions, inactivity stages |

| Operation | Mode | Process |

| Monthly participation quota scoring | cron | DP-048 |

| Outcome evaluation trigger | cron | DP-053 |
| Audit pool refresh | cron | DP-054 |

## Key rules

- Assignment weighting factors (FR-050): sphere-of-impact relevance, active competency domains, current workload (open assignment count), and a random component. No factor alone dominates.
- Each citizen's target load is 2-4 hours per month (FR-051). DP-052 checks for overloaded and zero-assignment citizens and rebalances.
- `participation_record` is written monthly by DP-048: `period` (YYYY-MM), `score` (voting + reviews + audit, weighted), `quota_target` (derived from jurisdiction memberships and competencies).
- Inactivity escalation (DP-049, FR-054): `inactivity_stage` steps from 0 (none) → 1 (reminder) → 2 (reduced assignments) → 3 (inactive). Stage 3 suspends advanced governance privileges but preserves constitutional protections and information access.
- `exemption_status` values (illness, disability, military, caregiving, other) pause civic obligations without penalty (FR-053). Exemptions require verification and are auditable.
- Audit pool refresh (DP-054, FR-055): monthly random selection of citizens into `civic_assignment` rows of type `audit_review`. Domain contributors are added as supporting participants without control authority.
- Participation score never alters voting weight (FR-052).
