## Responsibility

Manages voluntary, issue-specific, revocable, and auto-expiring vote delegations. Enforces liquid-democracy constraints: delegation is opt-in, domain-scoped, transparent, and cannot become permanent (ADR-008). Resolves delegation chains when a ballot is cast so that a delegate's vote counts for each citizen who delegated to them in that domain.

## Owned Tables

| Table | Role |
|-------|------|
| TBL-023 delegation | Primary owner — delegator → delegate mapping per domain with expiry |

## Operations

| Operation | Mode | Process |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Create delegation | sync | DP-014 |
| Revoke delegation | sync | DP-015 |
| Delegation chain resolution | async | DP-041 (triggered when a ballot is cast) |
| Delegation expiry enforcement | cron | DP-045 |

## Key rules

- A citizen may delegate in a `domain_id` only to a citizen with active competency in that domain (FR-056). Non-experts cannot receive delegations.
- Delegation is domain-scoped: a delegation in `healthcare` does not affect `transportation` or any other domain (FR-056).
- `revoked_at` is set on DP-015; the row is not deleted (audit trail). Revocation takes immediate effect.
- `expires_at` is mandatory. DP-045 runs daily and sets `revoked_at` on all delegations where `expires_at < now()` and `revoked_at IS NULL` (FR-057). No permanent delegates.
- DP-041 resolves the full delegation chain (delegator → delegate → further delegate) when a ballot is cast. Circular graphs are detected and rejected at creation time in DP-014.
- Delegation is publicly visible: delegator, delegate, domain, and period are readable by any citizen (FR-056).
- Audit event emitted on create, revoke, and expiry.

## Dependencies
