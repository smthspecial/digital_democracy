## Responsibility

Manages domain expert credentials, the multi-step competency acquisition pipeline, competency challenges, conflict-of-interest disclosures, and expert assessments. Competency is advisory only ‚Äî it never affects voting weight (FR-020). The service enforces domain independence, time-limited status, and automatic exclusion of conflicted contributors.

## Key rules

- Competency acquisition follows five ordered stages: application ‚Üí automated credential check ‚Üí public review period ‚Üí domain review ‚Üí recorded approval (FR-022). DP-031 drives each stage transition.
- `competency.status` transitions: `applied ‚Üí active` (after pipeline) or `rejected`; `active ‚Üí expired` (DP-044); `active ‚Üí revoked` (DP-032, challenge upheld).
- Expert assessments (DP-021) are blocked if the assessing citizen has an undisclosed `conflict_of_interest` in the relevant `domain_id` (FR-025). Checked at write time.
- DP-033 fires on every new `conflict_of_interest` row. It suspends any open `expert_assessment` submissions and active `governance_role` assignments in the conflicted domain (FR-063).
- Competency is domain-scoped: no cross-domain inference permitted (FR-021).
- `expert_assessment` scores are advisory only. No assessment can block or delay voting (ADR-007, FR-023).
- DP-044 runs daily at 02:00, sets `status=expired` for all past-`expires_at` active records, and enqueues DP-039 to notify holders (FR-026).
