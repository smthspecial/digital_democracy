## Responsibility

Delivers outbound notifications to citizens for all governance events that require awareness or action: new assignments, vote sessions opening and closing, proposal status changes, inactivity warnings, competency expiry, delegation updates, and blocked constitutional reviews. Is a pure consumer of other services' events; it holds no domain data.

## Owned Tables

None. Notification state (delivery status, read receipts) is stored in an internal notification store not modeled in the core domain schema.

## Operations

| Operation | Mode | Process |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Notification dispatch | async | DP-039 (consumed from notifications.dispatch queue) |

## Key rules

- All notifications originate from events published by other services to the `notifications.dispatch` queue. The notification-service never initiates events itself.
- Notifications are best-effort with a retry policy. A delivery failure must not block the upstream governance process that triggered it.
- Channels (email, push, in-app) are configurable per citizen. No channel is mandatory.
- Notification content must not expose private data: ballot choices, legal identity details, or government identifiers are never included in notification payloads.
- Vote session reminders (DP-046/C04 downstream) are dispatched when sessions open and at a configurable interval before they close (FR-044 context: informed participation).
- Inactivity notifications are dispatched by DP-049 at stage 1 (reminder) and stage 2 (reduced assignments).

## Dependencies

- Receives from: every service via `notifications.dispatch` queue
- External: email provider, push notification provider, in-app delivery
