## Description

Enable structured, evidence-linked deliberation and collective decision-making that produces legitimate outcomes despite permanent disagreement. Covers constraint-based governance, preference declaration, multi-option voting methods, structured disagreement, deadlock resolution, and outcome-based evaluation.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Deliberation is structured and arguments are linked to evidence, studies, budgets, and impact reports
- [ ] Proposals begin with shared constraints before solutions are debated
- [ ] Citizens evaluate multiple options rather than binary choices
- [ ] No governance process remains indefinitely blocked
