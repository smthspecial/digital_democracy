## Description

Protect fundamental rights from majorities. Certain principles (free speech, due process, equality, property, human rights) sit outside ordinary voting; changes to them require elevated thresholds and extended deliberation.

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Protected fundamental rights cannot be overridden by ordinary voting
- [ ] Constitutional matters require supermajority approval and elevated participation thresholds
- [ ] Constitutional review can block proposals that violate protected rights
- [ ] Democracy governs policy; the constitution governs boundaries
