# High-Level System Design

## Core Principle

Political authority originates from citizens and remains with citizens.

The system exists to coordinate collective decision-making, not to transfer sovereignty to permanent political representatives.

The role of institutions is to facilitate governance, provide expertise, execute approved decisions, and protect constitutional rights.

They do not own decision-making authority.

---

Citizen

Every public action follows this lifecycle.

# Layer 1: Citizen Identity

## Purpose

Guarantee one person = one civic identity.

### Requirements

* Verified identity
* Privacy protection
* Auditability
* Anti-fraud protection

### Citizen Profile

Each citizen has:

* Identity verification
* Place of residence
* Civic participation history
* Areas of declared expertise
* Voting history (private)
* Public contribution history

The system knows who can participate without exposing private voting behavior.

---

# Layer 2: Sphere of Impact

## Purpose

Ensure decisions are made by people affected by them.

Not every citizen votes on every issue.

Voting rights depend on impact.

### Examples

Street Lighting

Eligible voters:

* Residents of affected streets

City Metro Expansion

Eligible voters:

* Residents of affected city

Regional Water Infrastructure

Eligible voters:

* Residents of affected region

National Tax Reform

Eligible voters:

* Entire population

---

## Benefits

Reduces:

* voter fatigue
* uninformed participation
* political noise

Increases:

* relevance
* accountability
* local ownership

---

# Layer 3: Problem Registry

## Purpose

Create a structured method for identifying issues.

Any citizen can submit:

* problems
* opportunities
* improvement suggestions

Examples:

* dangerous intersection
* school overcrowding
* inefficient public service
* environmental concern

Each issue becomes publicly visible.

---

# Layer 4: Proposal Marketplace

## Purpose

Transform problems into solutions.

Multiple proposals can compete.

Example:

Problem:

Traffic congestion.

Proposals:

A) Build new road.

B) Expand public transit.

C) Modify traffic signals.

D) Mixed solution.

Citizens evaluate alternatives rather than voting on a single government proposal.

---

# Layer 5: Expert Review Layer

## Purpose

Provide knowledge without creating rulers.

Experts cannot approve policy.

Experts cannot block policy.

Experts provide:

* feasibility studies
* risk analysis
* cost estimates
* long-term projections

Every proposal receives:

### Technical Score

Can it work?

### Economic Score

Can it be funded?

### Social Impact Score

Who benefits?
Who is harmed?

### Sustainability Score

What are long-term effects?

Expert opinions are public.

Citizens retain final authority.

---

# Layer 6: Public Deliberation

## Purpose

Allow informed discussion before voting.

Discussion should be structured.

Not social media.

Not popularity contests.

Arguments are linked to:

* evidence
* studies
* budgets
* impact reports

Goal:

Improve proposal quality before voting.

---

# Layer 7: Budget Layer

## Purpose

Connect every proposal to financial reality.

No proposal enters voting without funding information.

Every proposal must display:

* cost
* funding source
* maintenance cost
* expected benefits

Citizens vote on both:

* policy
* spending

This prevents unlimited promises.

# Layer 8: Voting Layer

## Purpose

Convert public preferences into decisions.

### Decision Types

Local

Regional

National

Constitutional

Different thresholds apply.

Examples:

Local project:

Simple majority.

National infrastructure:

# Layer 9: Implementation Layer

## Purpose

Execute approved decisions.

Professional civil servants remain necessary.

Their role becomes:

* execution
* administration
* logistics

Not policy creation.

Government becomes an implementation organization rather than a decision monopoly.

---

# Layer 10: Public Oversight Layer

## Purpose

Track outcomes after approval.

Every approved project receives:

* timeline
* budget tracking
* milestone reporting
* outcome metrics

Citizens can observe implementation in real time.

---

# Layer 11: Civic Knowledge System

## Purpose

Improve decision quality.

Citizens receive:

* educational materials
* simulations
* impact models
* expert explanations

Before voting on highly technical issues.

The objective is informed participation.

Not gatekeeping.

---

# Layer 12: Delegated Expertise (Optional)

## Purpose

Reduce participation fatigue.

Citizens may temporarily delegate specific voting domains.

Examples:

Energy policy

Healthcare policy

Transportation policy

Agriculture

Delegation remains:

* voluntary

* issue-specific

---

# Layer 13: Constitutional Layer

## Purpose

Protect rights from majorities.

Certain principles cannot be overridden through ordinary voting.

Examples:

* freedom of speech
* due process
* equality before law
* property rights
* human rights

Democracy governs policy.

The constitution governs boundaries.

# Anti-Corruption Architecture

The system assumes corruption attempts will occur.

Therefore:

## No Hidden Budgets

All spending visible.

## No Anonymous Lobbying

Funding sources disclosed.

## No Permanent Political Class

Decision authority remains distributed.

## No Exclusive Access

All citizens have equal proposal rights.

## No Closed Deliberation

Discussions and analyses are public.

## No Untouchable Institutions

Every institution remains subject to public review.

---

# Governance Formula

The system can be summarized by a simple rule:

People closest to a problem identify it.

Citizens and experts collaboratively develop solutions.

Citizens directly decide priorities.

Institutions execute approved decisions.

Society continuously evaluates outcomes.

Power remains distributed, transparent, and accountable throughout the entire process.

# Distinction between decision rights and execution rights. 

Most governments mix these together. Your system becomes much more stable if citizens decide what should happen, while professional institutions decide how to implement it efficiently. That separation avoids turning every engineering, legal, or administrative detail into a public referendum.
